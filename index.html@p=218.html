Eco-friendly Construction Materials for Sustainable Apartments

Leading a sustainable life requires taking conscious decisions as well as mindful consumption. While choosing materials to build an apartment, options are aplenty to replace traditionally used materials with eco-friendly ones. Lets take a look at the options.

Recycled materials: Using recycled materials, whether it is wood, metal, or bricks is a great way to lessen the burden on the environment.

Low volatile organic compound paints: Volatile organic compounds contain carbon, methylene chloride, benzene, and formaldehyde and hence cause environmental pollution. Low volatile organic compound paints on the other hand contain less harmful components and are environment friendly.

Sustainable flooring options: Opting for locally sourced flooring options instead of imported ones such as Italian marble is good for the environment as well as the local economy. A few of the sustainable flooring options include locally sourced natural stones such as Kadappa stone; limestone, a sedimentary natural rock made of calcium carbonate; linoleum tiles, which are made of linseed oil, wood flour, tree resin, jute, and cork powder; and bamboo a recyclable as well as sustainable material.

In addition to these, precast construction practices are environment friendly for the following reasons: the slabs are built at the manufacturers site and whole sections are shipped to the construction site transportation related environmental impact is less. The manufacturing and assembling of these slabs take less energy thus they are more sustainable.

The city of Chennai houses a vibrant economy creating abundant job opportunities and thereby leading to a vast residential development in the city. Since the city epitomises an amalgamation of modern technology and rich cultural heritage, it is a favourable investment opportunity for homebuyers to buy a flat in Chennai.

Read on to know where you must invest in your perfect home in the city of Chennai.

Growth Destinations In Chennai

Chennai is home to over 11 million people, as of 2021 and is the fifth richest city in the country. With the automobile industry contributing significantly to the citys GDP, the economy is seeing corresponding growth. This also suggests that property prices will witness a stable rise and will continue to appreciate for many years to come. Even during the pandemic, there was a 92% rise in new project launches as compared to the previous year and sales reached the 50% mark as that of the pre-COVID-19 era. Hence, investing in your own home is a wise decision, but what are the emerging hotspots?

Residential Hotspots In West Chennai

There are quite a few options that are ideal for investment. Each of these localities offers a host of social amenities. Here, weve listed out the localities that have the highest appreciation:

Mount Poonamallee High Road And Pallavaram-Thoraipakkam Road

With IT and ITES sectors lining this part of the city, the locality is designed to house-wide, well-developed social infrastructure. The presence of tech parks is a great opportunity for those seeking a job and IT professionals to find apartments closer to work. Industrial hubs such as Ambattur and Avadi are just a stones throw away making it a clear winner for investors and end-users.

Pallikaranai-Medavakkam

Areas close to Old Mahabalipuram Road (OMR) and GST Road are also seeing a rise in demand for commercial and residential spaces. The strong IT development and good connectivity will continue to rope in investments for years to come.

Where To Buy Apartments In Chennai

The influx of people for its IT and financial services has made Chennai one of the most sought-after cities. West Chennai and South Chennai are amongst the top destinations for residential property. While Gudavanchery and Pallikarnai in the south are preferred locales, the west seems to be a hot favourite amongst home buyers. Places like Ambattur, Oragadam, and Mount Poonamallee, and Senneerkuppam are seeing an increase in demand forresidential opportunities.

Although set away from the hustle and bustle of the city, this serene locale offers brilliant connectivity to some of the citys finest educational institutions, SEZs and IT parks, reputed hospitals, and much more. Senneerkuppam is one of the few localities in Chennai that has a steady water table, which is one of the main criteria that a home buyer would consider, given that the city has faced acute water crisis in the past. All of these contribute towards the appreciative value of properties here.

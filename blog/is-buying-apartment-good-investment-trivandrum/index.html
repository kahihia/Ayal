Trivandrum Real Estate Market Overview

Kerala has experienced robust growth in recent years, driven by various factors, including remittances from NRIs & tourists, and excellent domestic spending & production. Trivandrum, in particular, has been at the forefront of this economic expansion. The city’s thriving job market and business opportunities have attracted a significant influx of highly skilled individuals.

An international airport within the city limits is a key contributor to Trivandrum’s soaring demand for residential real estate. This airport offers exceptional connectivity to major cities across India and key international destinations, especially in the Middle East, Europe, and North America.

Notably, Trivandrum airport also serves as a pivotal link for the neighbouring regions of South Kerala and, cities & districts in South Tamil Nadu, such as Kanyakumari, Nagercoil, and Tirunelveli – making it a crucial transportation hub for a large region.

Growth Prospects of the Trivandrum Real Estate Market

Trivandrum market has seen steady growth in recent years, and the trend is expected to continue in the upcoming years. Various factors are driving this growth, including the impact of the pandemic, upcoming project infrastructure, and expected trends in the years to come.

Investment Challenges & Risks for Investing in Trivandrum Apartments

Investing in apartments in Trivandrum, like any real estate market, comes with its fair share of challenges that buyers & investors should be aware of, including market fluctuations and legal considerations.

  1. Market Fluctuations:

    Property values can rise and fall due to economic conditions, demand-supply dynamics, and external factors. Investing in a market with volatile property prices can pose a significant risk. To mitigate this, it’s crucial to conduct thorough market research. Look for areas with steady, long-term growth, and diversify investments across different kinds of properties.

  2. Legal Considerations:

    Understanding and adhering to local real estate laws & regulations is vital. Unforeseen legal issues, disputes over property titles, or zoning changes can disrupt the investment. To navigate the complexities of real estate transactions and to ensure all documentation is in order, seek help from legal counsel.

  3. Mitigation Strategies:

    To minimise these risks, diversify your real estate portfolio across different geographical locations within Trivandrum. This spreads risk across multiple market conditions. Additionally, investing in the long term can help weather short-term market fluctuations. Engage with local real estate professionals and advisors who can provide insights into the local market, helping you make informed decisions.

Financing Options for Buying an apartment in Trivandrum

When considering buying an apartment in Trivandrum, understanding the various financing options available is crucial. Here’s a detailed look at some of the common avenues for financing apartment purchases:

  1. Home Loans:

    Banks and financial institutions offer home loans with varying norms and interest rates. Applicants must meet specific eligibility criteria, such as income, credit score, and property valuation. The loan typically covers a significant portion of the property’s cost, and the borrower repays it in equated monthly instalments (EMIs).

  2. EMI Calculations:

    Equated Monthly Instalments (EMIs) are critical to home loan repayment. EMI includes both the principal amount and interest. It is essential to calculate EMIs based on the loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure.

  3. Government Schemes:

    Government initiatives can substantially benefit apartment buyers. In India, the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is a notable scheme that provides interest subsidies and affordable housing options to eligible beneficiaries. State-specific methods and grants may also be available in Kerala, to support apartment buyers.

  4. Builder Financing:

    Some builders offer in-house financing options to buyers. While this can be convenient, comparing their terms with external lenders is essential to ensure you’re getting the best deal.

  5. Personal Savings:

    Another option is using personal savings or investments like Fixed Deposits, Provident Funds, or PPFs. This can reduce the loan amount required and, consequently the interest paid over the loan tenure.

FAQs

1. Is buying a flat or house better in Trivandrum?

Buying a flat is better in Trivandrum, as flats offer convenience, security, and amenities, making them suitable for those seeking a luxury lifestyle. Though houses provide more space & independence, the final decision depends on lifestyle, family size, and budget.

2. Is it worth buying a flat in Kerala?

It is worth buying a flat in Kerala, as they come with a lot of amenities and security. Kerala's real estate market has shown stability and growth, making it a promising place for investment in apartments.

3. Where is the best place to settle in Trivandrum?

Best places to settle in Trivandrum are Akkulam, Kowdiar, Sreekariyam and Vellayambalam. Each neighbourhood has its unique charm, so it is essential to consider factors like work, schools, and lifestyle when choosing the best place to settle.

4. Is Trivandrum an excellent place to settle?

Trivandrum is an excellent place to settle, as it is known for offering a high quality of life, excellent healthcare facilities, and cultural attractions. The city's greenery, pleasant climate, and well-planned infrastructure make it an appealing destination for residents.

5. Which floor is the best for buying a flat in Trivandrum?

Higher floors are the best for buying a flat in Trivandrum, due to better views and enhanced privacy. Lower floors are more suitable for those who want to avoid using elevators daily. The choice of floor depends on individual preference.

6. Is it expensive to live in Trivandrum?

No, it is not expensive to live in Trivandrum. Cost of living in Trivandrum depends on the lifestyle and choice of neighbourhood.

7. Which is better, Trivandrum or Kozhikode?

Choosing between Trivandrum and Kozhikode depends on your priorities. Trivandrum is the capital city, known for its government institutions, educational facilities, and cosmopolitan lifestyle. On the other hand, Kozhikode offers a more relaxed pace of life and is renowned for its historical & cultural heritage. The choice should align with your lifestyle and career objectives.

8. Is Trivandrum flood-prone?

Trivandrum is not flood-prone, but a few areas in the city are susceptible to flooding during heavy rains. The city has implemented measures to mitigate flooding. Residents must be aware of local conditions, choose safe locations, and stay informed about the city's flood management strategies to ensure their safety during monsoon.

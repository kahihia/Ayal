Why Trivandrum is the Best Place to Live in?

April 2nd, 2024

Trivandrum is the best place to live in India – due to presence of large IT parks, verdant greenery, optimal blend of tradition and modernity, cultural richness, emphasis on sustainability, and more.

Located on the west coast of India, Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala. A key IT hub of the state, it is also known as the ‘Evergreen city of India’ due to dense vegetation and low coastal hills. The city hosts many academic campuses, research centres, media institutions, and strategic establishments of the Government of India.

Trivandrum is also a tourism hub, with prominent temples, beautiful beaches, backwaters, and hills. With multiple benefits of living in Trivandrum, the city has been topping many surveys on quality of life.

A blend of tradition and modernity, Trivandrum is nestled along the serene Malabar Coast. It has a high quality of life, thanks to rich cultural heritage, lush greenery, and dedicated institutions for music and arts.

The city has excellent healthcare facilities, renowned educational institutions, and a booming IT sector with ample employment opportunities. Excellent planning ensures efficient transport and easy access to amenities.

The city’s low crime rates and welcoming atmosphere foster a sense of security and community. With its perfect harmony of nature, culture, and convenience, Trivandrum is the best city to live in India.

Rich Cultural Heritage of Trivandrum: India’s Best Place to Stay in

Once a prominent hub of the ancient Travancore kingdom, Trivandrum has majestic palaces like the Kowdiar and Kanakakkunnu palaces that showcase the region’s royal legacy. The city’s rich cultural tapestry is woven with Kathakali, a classical dance-drama form, and Koodiyattam, a traditional theatre art recognised by UNESCO. Swathi Thirunal College of Music and College of Fine Arts are renowned institutions based in the city, that have been enhancing learning in music and arts since decades.

While traditional festivals like Onam are celebrated with grandeur, the annual Padmanabhaswamy Temple festivals attract devotees from far and wide. The Attukal Pongala – where lakhs of women gather to offer prayers and cook a sweet dish made of rice – holds the Guinness World Record for being the largest congregation of women.

Trivandrum is renowned for its music concerts, literature festivals, and art exhibitions, fostering a dynamic cultural milieu. The Napier Museum and the Kuthiramalika Palace Museum showcase the region’s artistic heritage through their exquisite collections.

The city stands as a beacon of Kerala’s rich cultural heritage, where the seamless blend of tradition and modernity offers residents and visitors a glimpse into the soul of God’s Own Country.

Serene Natural Beauty: Why Trivandrum is Called Evergreen City of India

A plethora of natural wonders in and around the city captivate visitors and locals. Stunning beaches attract sun-seekers from around the globe; for example the Kovalam beach that is famous for its crescent-shaped shoreline and pristine waters. Varkala Beach enthrals with its dramatic cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, offering a unique seaside experience.

Lush greenery abounds in Trivandrum, with verdant parks like the sprawling Kanakakkunnu Palace grounds and the enchanting Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary that is home to diverse flora and fauna. The city’s gardens – such as the Jawaharlal Nehru Botanical Garden – showcase a vibrant array of tropical plants and trees, providing a serene escape amidst urban bustle.

Backwaters in nearby regions like Poovar and Vellayani, offer a tranquil retreat into nature’s embrace. Residents of the city can get a serene glimpse of Kerala’s backwater charm, as they cruise along the tranquil waterways flanked by swaying palms and quaint homes.

Scenic landscapes adorn the outskirts of Trivandrum, with destinations like Ponmudi offering breathtaking vistas of mist-covered hills and lush valleys. Agasthyakoodam, part of the Western Ghats UNESCO World Heritage Site, beckons trekkers with its pristine wilderness and panoramic views. From stunning beaches and lush greenery to tranquil backwaters and scenic landscapes, this natural bounty paints a picture-perfect canvas of Kerala’s unparalleled beauty.

Quality of Life: Why Trivandrum is Special

Often hailed as the jewel of Kerala, Trivandrum offers residents an exceptional quality of life – thanks to its robust infrastructure and amenities. The city is renowned for top-tier healthcare facilities, with prestigious institutions providing world-class medical care. A plethora of multispecialty hospitals and clinics ensure comprehensive healthcare coverage for residents.

Education opportunities abound, with esteemed institutions such as the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology and the University of Kerala offering quality higher education. Numerous renowned schools & colleges, and educational initiatives ensure a strong foundation for students.

Trivandrum’s reputation as one of India’s safest cities is well-deserved, with low crime rates and efficient law enforcement ensuring residents’ peace of mind. Its well-planned urban layout, pedestrian-friendly streets, and reliable public transport contribute to a sense of safety and security.

The city’s cosmopolitan ambiance, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant arts scene provide residents ample opportunities for recreation and cultural enrichment. Combined with abundant green spaces and proximity to scenic natural attractions, Trivandrum emerges as a modern city offering an unmatched quality of life.

Thriving Economy and Job Opportunities: Reasons to Invest in Trivandrum

Trivandrum’s economy is based on diverse sectors, offering abundant job opportunities and fostering robust growth. Anchored by Technopark and Infopark, the city’s expanding IT industry serves as a catalyst for innovation and entrepreneurship. These tech parks host a multitude of MNCs, startups, and IT firms, attracting skilled professionals and driving the city’s knowledge-based economy forward.

Its status as the state capital has ensured significant presence of government institutions and administrative offices, providing stable employment opportunities. Prestigious research organisations like the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC) have further contributed to the city’s scientific and technological prowess.

Trivandrum’s industrial landscape encompasses sectors such as tourism, healthcare, education, and manufacturing – offering a diverse range of career paths for its residents. The city also has a vibrant entrepreneurial ecosystem – with numerous incubators, accelerators, and support networks nurturing startups and small businesses.

Fuelled by its strong IT sector, government institutions, industries, and entrepreneurial spirit, Trivandrum’s dynamic economy continues to attract talent and investment and positions the city as a thriving employment hub in the region.

Culinary Delights

Trivandrum’s culinary scene is a delightful fusion of traditional Kerala cuisine, international flavours, and vibrant street food culture. At the heart of its gastronomic offerings lies the rich tapestry of Kerala’s culinary heritage, with dishes like appam, puttu, and seafood delicacies dominating menus across the city.

Fresh produce from the Arabian sea is a highlight of the city’s cuisine, with dishes like karimeen pollichathu (fish marinated in spices and grilled in banana leaves) and meen curry (fish curry) tantalising tastebuds with their unique flavours.

Trivandrum’s culinary landscape reflects its cosmopolitan character, with restaurants offering diverse cuisine options ranging from North Indian and Chinese to Continental and fusion dishes.

The city’s bustling street food scene is a gastronomic adventure in itself, with vendors dishing out savoury snacks such as parottas, dosas, and spicy chaats, as well as sweet treats like pazhampori and unniyappam.

Trivandrum’s markets and food bazaars have an array of fresh produce, spices, and local delicacies, providing residents and visitors with an immersive culinary experience. Whether savouring traditional favourites or exploring global flavours, residents are spoilt for choice with a tantalising array of culinary delights.

Community and Hospitality

Rooted in Kerala’s famed tradition of hospitality, Trivandrumites embody a spirit of kindness and inclusivity, welcoming visitors with open arms. The city’s close-knit neighbourhoods foster a sense of community where residents often extend helping hands and share bonds that go beyond mere friendship. Whether celebrating festivals together, participating in community events, or simply engaging in everyday interactions, residents cultivate connections that contribute to a vibrant social fabric.

Guests are welcomed and offered traditional refreshments, showcasing the city’s innate generosity and cordiality. Residents take pride in their heritage and cultural traditions, eagerly sharing them with tourists, visitors, relatives, and friends. Trivandrumites embrace diversity and promote inclusivity, enriching the social tapestry with their warmth and camaraderie. The city’s welcoming atmosphere, strong sense of community, and innate hospitality create a nurturing environment – making it a truly special place to call home.

Luxury Living: Which Area is Best to Stay in Trivandrum

Trivandrum offers a range of luxury housing options, catering to discerning residents seeking upscale living experiences. Posh areas such as Kowdiar, Vellayambalam, and Pattom are renowned for their opulent residences, characterised by spacious layouts, modern amenities, and meticulous attention to detail. These neighbourhoods have sprawling bungalows, luxurious villas, and high-rise apartments, often nestled amidst lush greenery and scenic landscapes.

Known for its stately mansions and elegant homes, Kowdiar stands out as one of the city’s most prestigious locales. Its tree-lined streets and tranquil surroundings offer a serene retreat for affluent residents. Similarly, Vellayambalam and Pattom feature upscale properties with stunning architecture and world-class amenities, catering to those with discerning tastes.

Luxury gated communities have redefined upscale living in Trivandrum, offering world-class amenities including clubhouses, swimming pools, sports facilities, landscaped gardens, and more. These residential developments prioritise privacy, security, and exclusivity – providing residents with a truly indulgent lifestyle. The luxury housing market here offers a diverse range of upscale residences in exclusive neighbourhoods, providing affluent residents comfort, elegance, and sophistication.

Sustainable and Eco-Friendly Environment

Trivandrum is committed to environmental sustainability, and has many initiatives aimed at preserving its natural beauty and promoting eco-friendly practices. The city prioritises green spaces, with expansive parks like the Kanakakkunnu Palace grounds and the Botanical Garden providing residents with serene retreats amidst urban landscapes. City authorities have undertaken tree-planting drives and biodiversity conservation efforts to enhance green cover and preserve local ecosystems.

Conservation efforts extend beyond parks, with initiatives focusing on preserving wetlands, mangroves, and wildlife habitats in and around the city. Organisations like the Thiruvananthapuram Zoo actively engage in species conservation and environmental education, raising awareness about the importance of biodiversity.

Trivandrum’s commitment to sustainability is also reflected in its transportation infrastructure, with an emphasis on eco-friendly modes of travel. Excellent planning, well-developed public transport, and promotion of electric vehicles & rental services have reduced carbon emissions and eased traffic congestion.

Trivandrum encourages sustainable practices in urban planning and development, incorporating energy-efficient building designs, waste management strategies, and renewable energy initiatives into city planning efforts. Through these holistic approaches, the city has been creating a greener, more sustainable urban environment for its residents and future generations.

Conclusion

There are multiple advantages of living in Trivandrum – thriving economy, rich cultural heritage, lush greenery, scenic tourism spots, and more. No wonder the city is the epitome of a balanced and fulfilling lifestyle. Apart from being a prominent IT hub, Kerala’s capital hosts numerous academic, research, and government institutions – ensuring ample job opportunities. Its top-tier healthcare facilities and leading educational institutions contribute to a high quality of life.

Trivandrum’s cultural richness is evident in its majestic palaces, vibrant arts scene, and traditional festivals. The city’s serene natural beauty includes stunning beaches, lush green parks, and tranquil backwaters. Residents also enjoy a wide array of culinary delights, from traditional Kerala cuisine to international flavours and street food delicacies.

The city’s strong sense of community, hospitality, and safety has created a welcoming atmosphere, making it a place where everyone feels at home. What’s more, Trivandrum’s commitment to sustainability is reflected in its conservation efforts, green spaces, and eco-friendly transportation options – ensuring a greener and more sustainable future. Trivandrum’s perfect blend of tradition and modernity, scenic landscapes, cultural richness, economic opportunities, and emphasis on sustainability, makes it the best city to live in India.

FAQs

1. What makes Trivandrum stand out as a place to live?

Trivandrum stands out as a place to live due to its rich cultural heritage, serene natural beauty, excellent quality of life, thriving economy, and strong sense of community.

2. Is Trivandrum a safe city to live in?

Yes, Trivandrum is a safe city to live in, for families and individuals.

3. Are there job opportunities available in Trivandrum?

Yes, there are many job opportunities available in Trivandrum, particularly in the expanding IT sector, government institutions, and various industries.

4. How is the cost of living in Trivandrum compared to other cities?

The cost of living in Trivandrum, compared to other cities, is on the lower side – with reasonable housing options and overall expenses.

5. What recreational activities are available in Trivandrum?

Recreational activities available in Trivandrum are exploring stunning beaches, enjoying vibrant arts and cultural scene, indulging in culinary delights, and experiencing traditional festivals and celebrations.

6. Is Trivandrum an environmentally friendly city?

Yes, Trivandrum is an environmentally friendly city, with various initiatives to promote eco-friendly practices such as green spaces, conservation efforts, and sustainable transportation options.

7. How is the education system in Trivandrum?

The education system in Trivandrum is excellent, with renowned institutions offering quality education at various levels. This makes the city an ideal place for families seeking educational opportunities for their children.

8. What transportation options are available in Trivandrum?

Transportation options available in Trivandrum are buses, taxis, auto-rickshaws, and the upcoming metro rail system. These ensure convenient and accessible travel within the city.

9. What healthcare facilities are available in Trivandrum?

Healthcare facilities available in Trivandrum are modern hospitals, clinics, and medical centres, providing residents with access to high-quality, world-class healthcare services.

10. How welcoming is the community in Trivandrum to newcomers?

The community in Trivandrum is highly welcoming to newcomers, fostering a sense of belonging and inclusivity.

11. Why Trivandrum is special?

Trivandrum is special due to its unique combination of verdant greenery, world-class amenities, and cultural heritage. Its vibrant arts scene, serene beaches, lush greenery, and warm hospitality make it stand out as a destination that offers both traditional charm and modern comforts.

12. Which area is best to live in Trivandrum?

The best areas to live in Trivandrum are Kowdiar, Vellayambalam, Sasthamangalam, Kumarapuram, and Akkulam. The best place to stay in Trivandrum depends on factors like proximity to workplaces, educational institutions, healthcare facilities, and personal preferences.

13. What are the interesting facts about Trivandrum?

Interesting facts about Trivandrum are: • The world’s richest temple, the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, known for its immense wealth and architectural grandeur. • Napier Museum that houses a rare collection of archaeological and historical artifacts. • One of the few cities in India with a coastline on the Arabian Sea and vicinity of the Western Ghats, offering diverse landscapes and ecosystems. • Ayurvedic treatments and wellness centres that attract health-conscious visitors from around the world. • Technopark is one of India's largest IT parks, and contributes significantly to the city's economy and reputation as a technology hub.

14. Why Trivandrum is called the Evergreen City of India?

Trivandrum is called the Evergreen City of India, due to lush greenery that remains verdant throughout the year owing to the region's tropical climate and abundant rainfall. The city's green cover – encompassing parks, gardens, and coconut groves – gives it a perennially vibrant and refreshing appearance, thus justifying its moniker.

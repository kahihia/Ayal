Best Places to Live in Pune

March 31st, 2023

Pune is a city located in the western state of Maharashtra, India. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and booming economy. The city is often referred to as ‘Oxford of the East’ due to the presence of numerous educational institutions.

Pune has emerged as one of the most popular places to live in India due to several factors. Firstly, it has a pleasant climate throughout the year, making it a comfortable place to reside in. Secondly, it is well-connected to major cities in India by road, rail, and air, resulting in seamless travel. Thirdly, Pune has a strong economy, with a focus on industries such as IT, manufacturing, and automotive. This has led to a thriving job market, attracting professionals from across the country.

Lastly, the premium residential areas in Pune offer a high quality of life, with excellent healthcare facilities, good infrastructure, and a variety of entertainment options, including cultural festivals, music concerts, and sports events. Additionally, the city has a rich history and culture, evident in its many landmarks, temples, and museums, which add to its charm and allure. All these factors make Pune a popular place to live in India.

Factors to Consider while Choosing a Neighbourhood

It is important to take your time and do your research to find an area that meets your needs and fits within your budget. Here are some of the key things you should think about while choosing the best place to live in Pune-

  • Proximity to School / Work Areas:

    One of the key factors to consider when choosing the best locality in Pune is how close it is to your child’s school, and your current / potential workplaces. This can help you save time and make your daily commute easier.

  • Safety:

    Look for neighbourhoods that have low crime rates and are well-lit at night. You can also check with local authorities or online forums to get a sense of the safety of a particular neighbourhood. Additionally, it is always a good idea to choose a gated community with access control and well-trained security personnel.

  • Amenities:

    The availability of amenities like grocery stores, restaurants, parks, and public transport can impact your quality of life. Look for areas that have a broad selection of these amenities, and are easily accessible.

  • Housing Costs:

    The cost of housing in a city can vary widely, so it is important to consider your budget while choosing a neighbourhood. Look for good localities in Pune that fit within your price range while still meeting your other criteria.

  • Infrastructure:

    When selecting a locale, consider the availability of public transport, roads, and other facilities. Also, check if there are any plans for infrastructure development or improvements in the neighbourhood.

9 Best Places to Live in Pune

  1. Koregaon Park

    Situated towards the south of the Mula-Mutha River, Koregaon Park is about 6 km from the city centre. The area is surrounded by lush greenery, and is known for its tree-lined streets, upscale shopping, dining, and nightlife options.

    Infrastructure and Amenities

    Koregaon Park is a well-developed area with good infrastructure and amenities. It has excellent connectivity to other parts of Pune and major cities in Maharashtra, with the Pune Railway Station and the Swargate and Shivaji Nagar Bus Stations located nearby.

    With the Pune International Airport just a 10-15-minute drive away, national and international travel is seamless. The area also has several reputed schools, colleges, hospitals, shopping malls, supermarkets, and high-end restaurants, making it a popular spot.

    Rent and Property Prices

    High property prices are a given in this posh residential area. While the average rent for a 2 BHK apartment ranges from INR 35,000 to INR 60,000 per month, the average price of a residential property is around INR 15,000 to INR 25,000 per square foot.

  2. Kalyani Nagar

    A well-planned and upscale locality that offers a comfortable and luxurious lifestyle to its residents, Kalyani Nagar is known for its greenery, quiet surroundings, and excellent connectivity to various parts of the city.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Kalyani Nagar offers a wide range of amenities, including malls, supermarkets, schools, hospitals, restaurants, and entertainment options. The area is also home to several parks and gardens, making it an ideal place for those who enjoy outdoor activities.

    Kalyani Nagar has excellent infrastructure, with well-planned roads and public transport facilities. The area offers easy access to the Pune International Airport and Pune Railway Station. The locality is also home to several commercial and business centres, making it an ideal location for professionals.

    Rent and Property Prices

    The average property prices in Kalyani Nagar vary depending on the size, location, and type of property. For a 2 BHK apartment, these range from INR 7,000 to INR 13,000 per sq ft. The average rent for a 2 BHK apartment in Kalyani Nagar ranges from INR 25,000 to INR 40,000 per month.

  3. Kothrud

    Kothrud is a popular residential area located in the western part of Pune. It is known for excellent connectivity, well-developed infrastructure, and abundant amenities, making it an attractive location for those looking for a comfortable and convenient lifestyle.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Kothrud is home to several reputed educational institutions, including the famous MIT College of Engineering and Cummins College of Engineering for Women. The area has several hospitals and healthcare centres, while also being well-served by a wide variety of supermarkets, shopping centres, retail outlets, food joints, and entertainment options.

    Through a robust network of roads and public transport services, Kothrud is well-connected to other parts of the city, including the railway station and the airport. The upcoming Chandani Chowk flyover and Pune Metro Aqua Line extension, will provide unparalleled connectivity to all areas of Pune and major IT hubs like Hinjewadi & Magarpatta.

    Average Rent and Property Prices

    Kothrud is one of the most premium residential areas in Pune, and the property prices in the area reflect that. The average rent for a 2 BHK apartment in Kothrud ranges from INR 25,000 to INR 35,000 per month. Depending on the property, location, and the amenities provided, the price can range from INR 9,000 to INR 14,000 per sq. ft.

    Projects offering luxury amenities like clubhouse, kids’ play area, fitness facilities, parking, swimming pool, are likely to cost much higher than this. A few other factors that influence the price of luxury flats in Kothrud are developer’s reputation, construction quality, architectural design, location of the project, and configuration of the flat.

  4. Baner

    Baner is a popular residential area located in west Pune. It is well-known for its beautiful landscapes, serene environment, and fast-developing infrastructure, making it an ideal location for those who wish to live in a peaceful yet well-connected area.

    Infrastructure and Amenities

    Baner has excellent infrastructure with well-maintained roads, footpaths, and streetlights. The area is also home to several prestigious schools, hospitals, shopping malls, restaurants, and other recreational facilities. Baner is well-connected to other parts of Pune via public transport, including buses and cabs.

    Average Rent and Property Prices

    The average rent for a 1BHK apartment in Baner can range from INR 10,000 to INR 25,000 per month, depending on the size and location of the property. The average property prices in Baner can range from INR 6,500 to INR 12,000 per square foot, depending on the location, amenities, and other factors.

  5. Shivaji Nagar

    Known for its bustling streets, markets, and cultural landmarks, Shivaji Nagar is located in the heart of Pune city. Here is a brief overview of the amenities, infrastructure, and property prices in Shivaji Nagar:

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Home to several supermarkets, malls, hospitals, restaurants, and entertainment options, Shivaji Nagar is a convenient location for residents. The area also has several parks and open spaces for outdoor activities. Shivaji Nagar is well-connected to other parts of Pune via public transport, including buses and trains. The area also has good road connectivity to major highways and expressways. There are several educational institutions located in Shivaji Nagar, including excellent colleges and schools.

    Average Rent and Property Prices

    The average rent for a 1 BHK apartment in Shivaji Nagar is around INR 15,000 to INR 20,000 per month, while the same for a 2 BHK apartment is INR 25,000 to INR 35,000 per month. The property prices in Shivaji Nagar vary depending on the location, size, and amenities. On an average, the cost of a 2 BHK apartment can be between INR 10,000 and INR 13,000 per sq ft.

  6. Aundh

    Aundh is a popular residential area located in the north-western part of Pune. The locality is known for its excellent infrastructure, amenities, and strategic location, which makes it one of the most preferred residential areas in Pune.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Aundh offers a wide range of amenities and infrastructure such as good schools, hospitals, supermarkets, malls, restaurants, cafes, and recreational facilities. The locality also has good connectivity to other parts of Pune, with easy access to major roads and highways.

    Average Rent and Property Prices

    The average rent for a 1 BHK apartment in Aundh ranges from INR 15,000 to INR 25,000 per month, while the rent of a 2 BHK apartment would range from INR 25,000 to INR 35,000 per month. In terms of property prices, a 2 BHK apartment can cost anywhere from INR 7,000 to INR 12,000 per sq ft.

  7. Viman Nagar

    Located in the north-eastern part of Pune, Viman Nagar is known for excellent infrastructure, strategic location, and proximity to Pune International Airport, making it a preferred choice among professionals and frequent travellers.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Viman Nagar offers a range of amenities that cater to the needs of its residents. The area has several shopping malls, including Phoenix Market City and Inorbit Mall. Additionally, there are many restaurants, cafes, and pubs in the area, making it a popular destination for foodies and nightlife enthusiasts.

    The area also has reputed educational institutions, including Symbiosis International University. Additionally, Viman Nagar has hospitals and healthcare facilities, including Ruby Hall Clinic and Columbia Asia Hospital. Transportation is another significant advantage of living here. The area is well-connected to other parts of Pune, with several bus routes and other public transport.

    Rent and Property Prices

    The average price of a 2BHK apartment in Viman Nagar ranges between INR 8,000 & INR 11,000 – depending on the locality and the amenities provided by the developer. The average monthly rent for a 2BHK apartment in the area is around INR 25,000 to INR 35,000.

  8. Erandwane

    Erandwane is a well-established residential area that is known for its peaceful and green surroundings, excellent connectivity, and proximity to major educational institutions, hospitals, and commercial hubs.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Erandwane is well-equipped with essential amenities and infrastructure such as hospitals, schools, colleges, supermarkets, shopping centres, restaurants, and parks. The locality also has good road connectivity with good public transportation available.

    Rent and Property Prices

    The rent and property prices in Erandwane vary depending on the type of property, location, and amenities. While the average price of a 2 BHK apartment in Erandwane ranges from INR 11,000 to INR 14,000, the rent for a 2 BHK apartment ranges from INR 20,000 to INR 35,000 per month.

  9. Katraj

    Katraj is a suburban area located in the southern part of Pune. It is primarily a residential area, known for its affordable housing options and proximity to several educational institutions, commercial areas, and healthcare facilities.

    Amenities and Infrastructure

    Katraj offers a decent standard of living, with essential amenities such as grocery stores, banks, hospitals, and pharmacies available in its vicinity. There are several parks and green spaces in the area, including the famous Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park.

    While the Pune-Satara Road passes through Katraj, the area is well-connected to other parts of Pune via public transport. The proposed metro line from Swargate to Katraj is expected to further improve connectivity in the area.

    Rent and Property Prices

    The average rent for a 2 BHK apartment is anywhere between INR 12,000-18,000 per month. The property prices in Katraj vary depending on the location, age, and amenities. A 2 BHK apartment can cost anywhere between INR 4000 to INR 7000 per sq ft.

Conclusion

Whether you are looking for a bustling commercial area or a quintessential Pune residential area, the city has something to offer to everyone. Pune combines the best of both worlds – temperate climate similar to that at hill stations, and conveniences that modern city life has to offer.

In areas like Kothrud, you can experience the serenity of NDA Hills yet stay connected with the infrastructures that matter – education, healthcare, work, and entertainment. Living close to nature ensures a peaceful, serene environment for your family, thus improving the quality of life.

Overall, Pune offers a range of places to suit different lifestyles and budgets. It is best to carefully consider your requirements before deciding on a place.

The Dramatic Transformation of Bangalore

Bangalore – once a quaint town, which was fondly called the pensioners’ paradise. Today, the city has earned itself a place on the global information technology map. In the last two decades, Bangalore’s growth has been burgeoning. Along with the IT boom started in the early 90s, young aspirants started flocking to Bangalore. The city became home to reputed IT parks and from across the world major IT companies started setting up their offices in Bangalore. In no time, Bangalore became the IT hub of the country and a dream destination for the young and vibrant.

High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNIs) and Luxury Homes

When people from all over the country started coming to Bangalore in large numbers, the city’s real estate market started growing exponentially, resulting in the emergence of apartments in large numbers. Today, Bangalore is rapidly upgrading its infrastructural facilities to cater to the ever-increasing demands. Thanks to the IT industry, Bangalore is home to a large number of HNIs, mostly working in executive positions. Quite naturally, they prefer luxury, especially when it comes to their home.
As a handy guide that is intended to provide you all the information you need to know about luxury apartments and row houses in Bangalore by SOBHA Ltd., India’s preeminent real estate organisation, let’s now take a look at Bangalore’s real estate market. Let’s delve deeper into the following: what makes Bangalore attractive for luxury home buyers, its IT sector, the growing economy of Bangalore, and the advantages of buying a property in the city.

Bangalore Real Estate Market

Sales of apartments in the first quarter of 2023 across seven major cities in the country have seen a 20% growth, with the number of apartments sold standing at 62,040 units. It is the highest sale achieved in a quarter in the last 15 years. Bangalore is leading the phenomenal growth with 21% market share – an indication of the potential the city holds for its real estate market. Among the total number of apartments sold in Bangalore, the luxury apartment segment’s contribution was about 15% – the demand for luxury housing in the city is steadily on the rise.

Rapidly Developing Infrastructure

Connectivity is a crucial factor that can be decisive in the future of a city. Ever since the IT boom, Bangalore has been witnessing a study influx of people. Obviously, the infrastructure needs to be upgraded to meet the growing demands. With the metro network progressing rapidly, connectivity across Bangalore will improve dramatically.
Besides the metro, there are other infrastructure projects, which would further improve connectivity. One of the most important projects among them is the Bengaluru Suburban Rail Project. The project will connect the entire city and will have four corridors: KSR Bengaluru City – Devanahalli, Baiyappanahalli – Chikkabanavara, Kengeri-Cantonment-Whitefield, and Heelalige-Rajanukunte. Expected to be completed by 2026, the suburban rail network will be 148.17 kilometre long.

Yet another project that is intended to boost Bangalore’s connectivity is the Peripheral Ring Road. Once completed, the 116-kilometre-long Peripheral Ring Road will ease the traffic on the Outer Ring Road, currently one of the busiest stretches in the city.

The Burgeoning IT Industry

In 1991, Bangalore’s software exports stood at a humble 16 crore. In 2022, the city, reinforcing its status as the IT capital of the country, earned Rs. 6.3 lakh crore from software exports – a giant leap, indeed!

Bangalore’s Booming Economy

As per Oxford Economics, Bangalore is the fastest growing city in the Asia-Pacific region with an expected growth rate of more than six percent in 2023. Home to the largest number of high-growth companies in India, the city is also considered as the leader in India’s biotechnology industry and the garment capital of the country. Since 2010, the number of tech startups launched in Bangalore stands at 9,346. Out of these 5,541 were launched in the last four years – an indication that Bangalore is the most preferred destination of aspiring entrepreneurs.

Investing in a Luxury Apartment – Appreciation and Return on Investment

Bangalore is projected to be one of the fastest growing cities in the world. Hence, investing in a luxury apartment in Bangalore is a no-brainer. Over the years, property prices in the city have been steadily moving upward. As per property consultant, Knight Frank India, in the third quarter of 2022, Bangalore’s property market witnessed the highest growth rate in the country. The trend is continuing and in the first quarter of 2023, Bangalore remans the leading contributor of residential real estate market sale in India. The average property price in Bangalore is approximately Rs. 5, 800 per square foot. However, the per square foot price for luxury apartments goes way beyond that. With massive year-on-year growth, there is no room for doubt that the best way you can make your money grow is to invest in a luxury apartment in Bangalore.
